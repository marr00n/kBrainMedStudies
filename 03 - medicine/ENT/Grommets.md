T-tube / Tympanostomy

[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ED1j9QcpEuI]

**Indications**
- recurrent OM (>3 episodes of acute OM over 6 months or >4 episodes over 1 year) with MEE at time of evaluation
- persistent effusion (>3 months)
- poor response to antibiotic regimen
- immunocompromised patient
- cleft palate
- impending complication (hearing loss)
- severe retraction pocket
- barotitis media
- autophony from eustachian tube dysfunction

**Procedure**
Supine, head turned away
Aim for Anterior inferior quadrant
Use EMLA or phenol

Large enough Myringotomy ~2mm
Suction ME space if fluid

Insert grommet with small alligator. Rest one side of flange in incision
Use small right angle, or pusher

Instill cipro HC drops, if required
NOTE GROMMET SERIAL NUMBER ON OP REPORT

**Consent**
Risks: persistent perforation (5%), post tube otorrhoea, bleeding, re-do
Granulation tisse

**Post op:**
- Dry ear precautions
- Simple analgesia
- Noise sensitivity for a few days
- No limits on mobility, diet, and head position
