**Defining terms**
Thromboplastin

* an enzyme responsible for converting prothrombin to thrombin (II)
* made of tissue factor + phospholipids

Partial thromboplastin

* Thromboplastin without tissue factor, i.e. just phospholipids

**Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time**

* Measure of the Intrinsic / Contact pathway
* Measure of time for thromboplastin to convert prothrombin to thrombin

Methodology

* Blood is collected with a calcium chelator (eg oxalate) which binds Ca2+ and thereby prevent coagulation
* Blood is centrifuged and plasma isolated
* Phospholipids (partial thromboplastin) is added to the sample (simulating damaged endothelial surface) with an excess of calcium to recommence coagulation
* Clotting time is measured and reported

Clotting factors

* XII, XI, IX and X, kallikrein, kinin

Causes of elevated APTT

* Inherited
	* Haemophillia B (IX) and Heamophilia A (VIII)
	* von Willebrand's disease (carrier of factor 8, lack of vWF means VIII degrades quickly)
	* Factor XII and XI deficiency
	* Deficiency of Kallikrein, Kinin
	* Antiphospholipid syndrome
		* Lupus anticoagulant (immunoglobulin/antibody that binds to phospholipids)
		* Anticardiolipin (antibody that binds to cardiolipin - a type of phospholipid found mainly in mitrochondrial wall)
* Acquired
	* Heparin
	* Severe liver disease - decreased production of clotting factors
	* Warfarin (big dose)

Assessing isolated elevated APTT

* Repeat test to ensure not spurious
* If fibrinogen is normal, excludes DIC
* Mixing study - excludes clotting factor deficiency
* Then, test for individual clotting factors/immunoglobulins accordingly
