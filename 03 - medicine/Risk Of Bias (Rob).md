High Risk of Bias leads to loss of internal validity
#### Selection Bias 
Randomisation
- Alternate days / birthday basis are all too predictable
- Random computer generated is better, but this generation must be secret. Otherwise people will know what the next allocation is and can decide to recruit or not recruit
	- E.g. telephone based or web based allocation, opaque sealed envelope
#### Performance Bias
- Occurs when investigator and/or patient is unblinded to the study.
- e.g. a patient receiving a drug for weight loss is happy to be receiving it and so doesn't do usual standard care (e.g. diet and exercise), consequently the drug appears to do worse than standard care
- Blinded participants more likely to comply, 

####  Measurement / Detection Bias
- Bias upon the measurement taken

#### Attrition Bias
- Missing outcome data

#### Selective Reporting Bias
- Omitting findings that were found in the publication.

#### Other Biases
- Trial prematurely ended
- Industry sponsorship

### Reporting Bias in your Systematic review
- Risk of Bias can reported for each individual article across domains
- Should be reported for each outcome
- Can be presented with forrest plot and ROB graph.
- Different types of ROB tools are required for each study design
	- See [EQUATOR Network](http://www.equator-network.org) reporting guidelines
	- [JBI.global](JBI.global) has lots of checklists for assessing studies
	- Each type of study design must be assessed according to the tool of that study design
