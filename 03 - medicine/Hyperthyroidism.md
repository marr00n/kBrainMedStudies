## Primary Hyperthyroidism

[Great video by Nerd Ninja](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=epWJ2v5qy5E&t=6s)

Excess production of T3 / T4 by the thyroid follicular cells. Either due to:
1. hyperfunctioning TSH receptors, or
2. over-stimulation of TSH receptors, or
3. exogenous thyroid hormone.

Effect: ↑ T<sub>3</sub>/T<sub>4</sub> = ↓ TRH from hypothalamus = ↓ TSH from ant pituitary

### A. Hyperfunctioning thyroid
Typically permanent thyrotoxicosis
Release of thyroid hormone hyperactive follicular cells.
#### Graves Disease
- most common cause of hyperthyroidism
- Cause
	- HLA-DR3 or HLA B8 mutation → hyperactivity from plasma (MH2) cells → TSH-receptor auto-antibodies
- TSH-R antibodies cause diffuse destruction of all follicles of thyroid gland
#### Thyroid Adenoma
- 10:55 of video
- Benign mass of follicular tissue
- ↑ TSH-receptor sensitivity or auto-activation

#### Toxic multinodular goitre
- multiple benign massess i.e. adenoma

#### Jod Basedow Phenomenon
- Have one of the above conditions, give high dose Iodine (e.g. contrast radioactive Iodine, or amiodarone)
- Trigger for thyroid storm

### B. Thyroid Destruction
Typically transient thyrotoxicosis
↑↑ thyroid hormone AND thyroglobulin from colloid of destroyed follicular cells.
### Hashimoto's
- Anti-TPO & Anti-TG antibodies leads to autoimmune destruction of the follicular cell reaction
- Initial hyperthyroid state.
- Eventually enter <u>chronic</u> hypothyroid state due to ongoing antibody production and subsequent inflammation / destruction of follicular cells
### Postpartum
- Anti-TPO antibodies produced from pregnancy
- Destruction of follicular cells =  ↑ transient thyrotoxicosis, then hypothyroid, then recover to euthyroid
### Dequervain's 18:05
- Triggered by viral infection (often URTI) → direct cytotoxic damage to follicles
- Destruction of follicular cells =  ↑ transient thyrotoxicosis, then hypothyroid, then recover to euthyroid
- Presentation
	- painful due to direct injury, also ↑ ESR

## Secondary Hyperthyroidism
Youtube video 21:36

Hypothalamus or pituitary dysfunction leads to ↑ TSH and ↑ T<sub>3</sub>/T<sub>4</sub>
#### Pituitary Adenoma
- Small tumour of the pituitary that produces excess
- Can:
	- ↑ TSH
	- ↑ Prolactin
		- F: galatorhoea, menstrual irregulariteis, reduced libido
		- M: gynaecostmastia
	- ↑ ACTH
		- Causes Zona Fasiculata to ↑ cortisol = cushing's disease
	- ↑ GH
	- Acromegally Muscles, liver



## Pathology & Presentation

#### Goitre
- Enlargment due to hypertrophy and hyperplasia from
	- TSH stimulation (secondary hyperthyroidism)

#### Metabolic Dysfunction
- hyperactivate Na/K ATPase = ↓ATP = ↑ catabolism
	- weight loss, hunger, excess heat (heat intolerance, diaphoretic)

#### Neurological Dysfunction
- i.e. Hyperactive neuronal activity
- Hyperactive behaviour - Agitation, anxiety, insomnia, seizures
- ↑ Deep tendon reflexes 

#### Cardiovascular Dysfunction
- T<sub>3</sub> /T<sub>4</sub> binds to ↑ β<sub>1</sub> receptor sensitivity
	- ↑ HR and contractility = ↑ cardiac output
- Presents: Atrial fib or SVT;  *systolic* hypertension
- ↓ SVR
