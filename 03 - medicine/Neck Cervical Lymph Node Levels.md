### Landmarks
- **Bone**
	- Mandible & mastoid
	- Hyoid
	- Clavicle
- **Muscles**
	- SCM - from mastoid to clavicle
	- Trapezius - posteriorly
	- Digastric - from chin to hyoid, and then hyoid to mastoid
	- Omohyoid - from hyoid to top of scapula
		- intermediate tendon is anchored to the clavicle and first rib by a fascial sling.

### Neck Level Classification and Border Changes

#### Level I: Submental and Submandibular Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Anterior:** Midline of the neck
  - **Posterior:** Posterior edge of the submandibular gland (new border)
  - **Superior:** Mandibular body
  - **Inferior:** Hyoid bone

- **Level IA (Submental) vs. Level IB (Submandibular)**
  - **Dividing Landmark:** Anterior belly of the digastric muscle  

- **Changes:**
  - The **posterior edge of the submandibular gland** now serves as the landmark to separate **Level IB from Level IIA** instead of the **stylohyoid muscle** (improves imaging clarity). Styloidhyoid may still be used for surgical identification

---

#### Level II: Upper Jugular Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Anterior:** Posterior edge of the submandibular gland (new border)
  - **Posterior:** Posterior border of the sternocleidomastoid (SCM) muscle
  - **Superior:** Skull base
  - **Inferior:** Hyoid bone (dividing line between Levels II and III)

- **Subdivision:**
  - **Level IIA vs. Level IIB**
    - **Dividing Landmark:** Spinal accessory nerve (CN XI)  
    - **IIA:** Nodes located **anterior or medial** to the spinal accessory nerve.  
    - **IIB:** Nodes located **posterior or lateral** to the spinal accessory nerve.

- **Changes:**
  - The **posterior edge of the submandibular gland** replaces the **stylohyoid muscle** as the anterior border for Level IIA.

---

#### Level III: Middle Jugular Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Anterior:** Medial aspect of the common carotid artery (new imaging landmark)
  - **Posterior:** Posterior border of the SCM
  - **Superior:** Hyoid bone (dividing line between Levels II and III)
  - **Inferior:** Cricoid cartilage (dividing line between Levels III and IV)

- **Changes:**
  - New imaging landmark: **Medial aspect of the common carotid artery** is now used to define the **medial border of Levels III and IV**, replacing the **sternohyoid muscle**, but the sternohyoid remains the intraoperative reference as it is more superficial.

---

#### Level IV: Lower Jugular Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Anterior:** Medial aspect of the common carotid artery (new imaging landmark)
  - **Posterior:** Posterior border of the SCM
  - **Superior:** Cricoid cartilage (dividing line between Levels III and IV)
  - **Inferior:** Clavicle

- **Changes:**
  - As with Level III, the **medial aspect of the common carotid artery** is now used as an imaging-based landmark instead of the **sternohyoid muscle**. But the sternohyoid remains the intraoperative reference as it is more superficial.

---

#### Level V: Posterior Triangle Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Anterior:** Posterior border of the SCM
  - **Posterior:** Anterior border of the trapezius
  - **Superior:** Skull base
  - **Inferior:** Clavicle

- **Subdivision:**
  - **Level VA vs. Level VB**
    - **Dividing Landmark:** Inferior border of the cricoid cartilage  
    - **VA:** Nodes **superior** to the inferior border of the cricoid cartilage.  
    - **VB:** Nodes **inferior** to the inferior border of the cricoid cartilage.

- **Changes:**
  - No major changes in anatomical definitions.

---

#### Level VI: Anterior (Central) Compartment
- **Borders:**
  - **Lateral:** Medial aspect of the common carotid artery (new imaging landmark)
  - **Superior:** Hyoid bone
  - **Inferior:** Suprasternal notch

- **Changes:**
  - The **medial aspect of the common carotid artery** is now an imaging-based reference for the **lateral border**, replacing the **sternohyoid muscle**. But the sternohyoid remains the intraoperative reference as it is more superficial.

---

#### Level VII: Superior Mediastinal Nodes
- **Borders:**
  - **Superior:** Suprasternal notch (dividing line between Levels VI and VII)
  - **Inferior:** Innominate artery

- **Changes:**
  - Clarification that **Level VII extends only to the level of the innominate artery**, not below.
  - Surgeons may still refer to these nodes as **superior mediastinal lymph nodes** rather than Level VII.

---

### **Key Changes in Borders**
| **Neck Level** | **Previous Landmark** | **New Landmark** |
|--------------|------------------|------------------|
| IA vs. IB | None previously clarified | Anterior belly of the digastric muscle |
| IB vs. IIA | Stylohyoid muscle | Posterior edge of the submandibular gland |
| IIA vs. IIB | None previously clarified | Spinal accessory nerve (CN XI) |
| III & IV vs. VI | Sternohyoid muscle | Medial aspect of the common carotid artery |
| VA vs. VB | None previously clarified | Inferior border of the cricoid cartilage |
| VI vs. VII | Not clearly defined | Suprasternal notch as superior border of Level VII |

These refinements provide **greater clarity for imaging-based classification** while maintaining **surgical practicality**. Let me know if you need any further modifications!
