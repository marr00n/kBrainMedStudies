## Definition
Volatile anaesthetics are gases used to induce a or maintain a state of anaesthesia.

Some examples are desfluorine, isoflurine, and sevoflurine and nitrous oxide.

## Concentration and Delivery

The concentration of the gas is expressed as a percentage of its volume to the total volume of the gas mixture.

Modern anaesthetic machines are able to precisely vaporise the liquid inhalational agent in order to deliver very accurate concentrations to the patient.

## Mechanism of Action

Not fully known, likely multifactorial.

Volume Expansion Theory
- Gas dissolves in lipid cell membranes, distorting them, and impacting sodium channels needed for synapse transmission

Neurotransmitter interference
- Acts at pre-synaptic and post-synaptic receptors interfering with the release and reuptake of various neurotransmitters thereby altering synapse transmission

Activation of [GABA](GABA.md)
- Activate GABA receptors inhibiting the calcium channel release and reduce neurotransmission
