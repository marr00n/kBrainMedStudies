## Mechanism of Action

- Agonist of the mu-opioid receptor

## Efficacy

## Side Effects

- Opioid induced ventilatory impairment (respiratory depression)
- [Post-Operative Nausea & Vomiting (PONV)](01%20-%20Anaesthetics/Post-Operative%20Nausea%20&%20Vomiting%20(PONV).md)
- Miotic effect (pupillary constriction) on eyes
- Constipation & Urinary retention
- Pruritus

## Antidote

- [[Naloxone]]

## Specific Drugs

### Morphine

- Morphine-6-glucuronide - Active metabolite
- Morphine-3-glucuronide - thought to cause convulsions
- Renally excreted
- Releases histamines causing itch and wheals

### Fentanyl

- 100x more potent than morphine
- Yields its effects in ~6 minutes
- Lipid soluble
- Causes euphoria
- Has a non-active metabolites, which accumulates in renal failure, but because non-active can be used in CKD.

### Oxycodone

- 1.3x more potent than morphine
- Favoured oral agent for PRN pain relief
- Constipation
- Highly abused

### Hydromorphone

- 5x more potent than morphine
- No active metabolites
- Euphoric
- Often used in opioid tolerant patients

### Buprenorphine

- Partial agonist of mu-receptor
- Given as PO, SL, or Top - means lot so of options

### Tramadol

- Oral IR and MR formulations
- Acts via mu-receptor, serotonin agonism, [Norepinephrine Reputable Inhibitor](01%20-%20Anaesthetics/Norepinephrine%20Reputable%20Inhibitor.md)

### Methadone

- 2-10x more potent than morphine
- Used in addiction medicine as its long acting nature minimises cravings & withdrawal symptoms and therefore compulsive behaviour. Additionally, oral formulation reduces IVDU risk.
- Associated with QT prolongation
- Has [NMDA Receptor](01%20-%20Anaesthetics/NMDA%20Receptor.md) antagonism

### Oxymorphone

- 3-10x more potent than morphine
- Euphoric
- No active metabolities

### Tapentadol

- 0.5x potency of morphine
- Opioid combined with [[01 - Anaesthetics/Norepinephrine Reputable Inhibitor]]

### Codiene

- uncommonly used due to genetic factors that effect metabolism.
	- Ineffective in 10% of people
