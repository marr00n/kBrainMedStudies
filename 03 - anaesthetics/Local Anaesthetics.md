## Mechanism of Action

- Block voltage gated sodium channels, by binding to the alpha subunit.
- By preventing sodium influx, the nerve is [[hyperpolarised]] reducing the likelihood of generating and conducting an [[action potential]].

## pH and LA

- LAs are weak bases
- pKA is the pH at which 50% of the drug is in ionised form. An ionised LA is less lipid soluble and slower to penetrate tissue
- Most LAs have a pKA of 8 or higher, and therefore an infected (anaerobic, acidic environment) results in poor results

## Cardiotoxicity

- Lidocaine is the safest
- Bupivocaine (Marcaine) and Ropivocaine are contraindicated in IV regional anaesthesia.
