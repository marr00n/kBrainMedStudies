MAC is the alveolar concentration of a volatile agent at which 50% of patients do not move in response to a standard surgical stimulus at 1 atmosphere.

The MAC ratio or the MAC multiple describes how to increase the concentration of the inhalational agent to increase the probability of a patient not moving.

For example, MAC 1.3 has 97.5% of the population not moving.
