## Indications
- Shoulder surgery: arthroscopic procedures, rotator cuff repair, acromioplasty, stabilisation, arthroplasty

## Contraindications
- ⚠ Severe respiratory disease or ↓ respiratory reserve
	- ipsilateral phrenic nerve block occurs in up to 100% of cases; even 25% ↓ in FVC may be poorly tolerated
- ⚠ Contralateral phrenic nerve palsy
- Contralateral pneumothorax

> [!Tip] Assess FEV or FVC
> If FVC or FEV are borderline, consider a different block e.g. [[Suprascapular Nerve Block]] or [[Axillary Nerve Block]], 


## Anatomy
- **Brachial plexus roots** (C5–T1) exit between scalenus anterior and scalenus medius
- Roots C5, C6, C7 targeted; classic ultrasound appearance = **"traffic lights" pattern** in the *interscalene groove*
	- C7 transverse process: absent anterior tubercle; vertebral artery and vein visible at this level
	- C6 transverse process: prominent anterior tubercle = Chassaignac's tubercle
	- Muscle bridge between C7–C8 commonly impairs imaging and LA spread to C8–T1 → ulnar nerve sparing common (ulnar border of hand and ring/little fingers may remain sensate)
- **Phrenic nerve** (C3–5) lies immediately anterior to scalenus anterior — at risk with any approach

## Technique
### Supine positioning
- [Interscalene Block Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KzGysLytNdU)
- Infiltrate about 10mls

Note: posterior port of arthroscopic procedures may fall outside block territory (T2 fibres) → may require supplemental LA infiltration by surgeon
