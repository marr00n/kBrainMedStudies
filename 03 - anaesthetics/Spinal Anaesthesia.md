## Spinal Anaesthesia

### Indications
- Emergency & elective operations below the umbilicus

### Anatomy
- Aiming for CSF space, which is the subarachnoid space
- Cauda equina starts at L1/2, Here the nerve roots are only covered by pia mater

### Physiology of CSF
- Baricity is a ratio of drug fluid density to CSF density

#### Effects
- Nervous:
- Respiratory:
- Cardiovascular:
- Gastrointestinal:

### Technique


Same as [Epidural Anaesthesia](../Epidural%20Anaesthesia.md)
But ensure you do **barbotage** – aspirate CSF and reinject during spinal anaesthesia to confirm position and enhance distribution.

#### Assessment of Block
##### Bromage Motor Block Scale

1. **No block**: full flexion of knees & feet  
2. **Partial block**: just able to flex knees, full flexion of feet  
3. **Almost complete**: unable to flex knees, some foot flexion  
4. **Complete**: no movement
