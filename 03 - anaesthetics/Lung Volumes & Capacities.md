## Lung Volumes

| Volume                             | Description                                               | Average | Notes                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tidal volume**                   | Volume that enters and leaves with quiet breathing        | 0.5L    | Changes with pattern of breathing e.g. shallow breaths vs deep breaths<br><br>Increased in **pregnancy**                                                              |
| **Inspiratory reserve volume**     | Extra volume that can be inspired with forced inspiration | 2.5L    | Relies on muscle strength, lung **<u>**compliance**</u>** (elastic recoil) and a normal starting point (end of tidal volume)                                          |
| **Expiratory reserve volume**      | Extra volume that can be expired with effort              | 1.5L    | Relies on muscle strength and low **airway** **resistance**<br><br>Reduced in **pregnancy**, obesity, severe obstruction or proximal (of trachea/bronchi obstruction) |
| **Residual volume/reserve volume** | Volume remaining after maximum expiration                 | 1.5L    | Cannot be measured by spirometry                                                                                                                                      |
| Dead Space                         |                                                           |         |                                                                                                                                                                       |

### Dead Spaces
1. **Anatomical (serial) dead space** is the volume of air that never reaches alveoli.
	- From mouth to terminal bronchioles.

2. **Alveolar (distributive) dead space** is the volume of air that reaches alveoli but never participates in respiration.
	- Alveoli that are ventilated but not perfused e.g. due to pulmonary embolus.

## Capacities

| Capacity                                 | Description                                                  | Expression                                                            | Average | Notes                                                                                                              |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Vital capacity/forced vital capacity** | Volume that can be force inspired after a forced expiration. | Inspiratory reserve volume + tidal volume + expiratory reserve volume | 4.5L    | Often changes in disease<br><br>Requires adequate <u>**compliance**</u>, muscle strength and low airway resistance |
| **Inspiratory capacity**                 | Max inspired after regular expiration                        | Tidal volume + inspiratory reserve volume                             | 3L      |                                                                                                                    |
| **Functional residual capacity**         | Volume remaining after quiet expiration                      | Expiratory reserve volume + residual volume                           | 3L      | Affected by height, gender, posture, changes in lung compliance. Height has the greatest influence.                |
| **Total lung capacity**                  | Volume of air in lungs after maximum inspiration             | Sum of all volumes, including residual volume                         | 6L      | Restriction < 80% predicted<br><br>Hyperinflation > 120% predicted<br><br>Measured with **helium** **dilution**    |

## Measuring Volumes & Capacities
### Simple Spirometry
- measures how much air you move in and out during normal and deep breathing
- Cannot measure residual volume (air left in lungs after full exhalation).
- Values are adjusted for height (largest influence), age, and sex.
- Breathe into a closed, oxygen-filled system moves a weight within a chamber up and down, and this movement is recorded to show how lung volume changes with each breath over time.

### Helium Dilution
- Estimates total lung capacity
 - After a normal expiration, the subject inhales a known concentration of helium, holds their breath to allow mixing, and the degree of dilution is used to calculate ventilated lung volume.
 - Accurate only if all lung regions are ventilated. Obstructed/under ventilated areas result in under-estimates.

### Nitrogen Washout
- Estimates anatomical (conducting airway) dead space up to and including the terminal bronchioles.
- Inhale pure oxygen and exhale. Measures how much pure oxygen is received back as this is the non-alveolar (dead space) volume. Once nitrogen starts to be detected, this represents alveolar gases
