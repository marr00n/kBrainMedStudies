## Apfel’s Criteria for Prophylaxis
- Baseline risk with zero risk factors = 10%
- Then, 1 point each for Female gender, Non-smoker, Hx of PONV or motion sickness, Post-operative opioids
- Number of risk factors:
	1. 20% risk of PONV
	2. 40% risk
	3. 60%
	4. 80%

## Approach
- each antiemetic reduces risk by 20%
- For very high risk patient, use 3 agents of different classes
- For high risk, use 2. Typically dexamethasone and a -setron
- For rescue, use a different class

## Dexamethasone
- Steroid medication  
- Contraindicated in [[diabetes]]
- Unknown mechanism of action (theories exist)  
- Typically **4–8 mg IV**, **TDS / PRN**

## Ondansetron & Granisetron
- [5-HT3 receptor](5-HT3%20receptor) antagonists
- 1mg stat for Granisetron
- side effects: headache, constipation
