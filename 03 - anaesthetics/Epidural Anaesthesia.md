Definition: Chemical transaction of cord

## Indications & Contraindications

### Indications
- Lower abdo surgery
- Chronic pain

### Contraindications
- No consent
- Local skin Septis
- Coagulaopathy. Want Plt > 100. Fixed by laminectomy
- High ICP if you convert epidural into a spinal

### Relative Contraindications
- bad sepsis
- Blood thinners
	- Aspirin usually ok
- HTN or pre-eclampsia at risk of causing sympatholysis and crashing BP
- stenotic aortic or mitral valves. As preload crashes and makes it easier
- Same for hypotension
- Pre existing Neurological deficits
- Previous back injury
- Unable to follow commands
- Increased ICP

## Physiological Effects
- CNS
	- motor at same level. Sensory block 2-3 levels higher come below, and SNS block 4-6 levels higher as nerves comes very low
	- Vasodilation from SNS block
	- Negative effect on cardiac output. Block to T4 of heart
	- If we add other meds can have effects.
- CVS
	- Decreased preload, contractility
	- vasodilation
- Resp
	- Phrenic won’t be blocked but intercostal muscles are blocked. Subjective feelings of SOB.
- Temp
	- extensive body heat redistribution due to vasodilation
	- 1-1.5 deg core temp loss.
	- Can’t shiver lower limb to warm up

## Anatomy of Epidural Space
- real space
- Boundaries
	- Cranium to S2 where dura ends
	- Dura to ligamentuk flava, btwn 2 layers of dura (spinal and osseous)
- Contents
	- fat nerves
- Landmarks to identify the vertebral levels
	- Spinal cords end at L1/2
	- Aim needle L3/4
	- Scapula tip at T7
	- PSIS at S2
- Layers
	- Skin, fat, supraspinous ligament, interspinous ligament, ligamentum flavum (pop sensation, feels like pushing through rubber), epidural space, dura

## Complications
- Post dural puncture headache, after 24 hours. Lasts 1 week.
- Local anaesthetic systemic toxicity
- Anaphylaxis
- Wrong drug, wrong mode of delivery
- High level
- Abscess / haematoma
- Pneumocephalus
- Permanent neurological injury

Uterus and Cervix T10 to L1

Vagina and perineum S2-4

## Procedure

### Consent
- headache 1:100-200, from dural puncture
- Injection and bleeding 1:1000-2000
- Nerve damage
	- Temp 1:3000
	- Prolonged 1:15,000
	- Permanent <1:100,000
- Poor or patchy block 1:50, especially vagina and perineum
- High block
- Hypotension and fetal brachytherapy
- Allergy
- Intravascular feed - replace

### Equipment

-16 or 18G Tuohy needle

	- 16 gives better feel as thicker

	- 18 is smaller and less chance of dura puncture

- needle is 8cm of metal and 9.5cm to the plastic base

### Procedure
- mark landmarks
- sterile prep
- Gown and glove
- prime line with saline with filter attached
- midline and direct needle into back
- Once needle is stabilised, remove stylet and aspirate/advance.
- leave 3-5cm in the subdural space + depth to dural space = length of catheter in body
- Apply lock dressing but don’t stick
- Tests for location
	- gravity to see if CSF or blood (should be nothing). Might get temporary of PA
	- meniscus test - epidural space has negative pressure. So should see the primed line level decrease (but only while woman not contracting)
	- Aspiration test - aspirate gently
	- Test dose - inject 5ml through
	- Check if intrathecal or intra vascular. If intrathecal will get very quick (2-3 minute) loss of motor.
- Stick dressing when happy.
- Make a loop for protection and apply epidural sticker
- Apply

#### Documentation
- redcap form
- Meds:
	- Stat: esomeprazole 40mg PO now (in case GA needed), metoclopramide 10mg IV if NELSCS
	- Naloxone 50mcg IV q30min for itch.
	- Ondansetron 4-8mg q6h for nausea

#### Rule of 5s
- catheter 5cm in space
- 5ml test dose
- Bolus 5ml, 5 mins apart

#### Monitoring
- [Dermatomal Assessment](01%20-%20Anaesthetics/Dermatomal%20Assessment.md) to ensure blocked. Check bilaterally.
- q5 BP for 20 minutes post
- Maternal HR
- CTG for fetal brady
