Intraoperative monitoring encompasses continuous assessment of oxygenation, ventilation, haemodynamic status, anaesthetic depth, neuromuscular blockade, and end-organ perfusion. Vigilance and interpretation of these parameters are core clinical responsibilities throughout every anaesthetic.

---

## Pulse Oximetry

SpO<sub>2</sub> is based on differential light absorption by oxyhaemoglobin (oxyHb) and deoxyhaemoglobin (deoxyHb). Red and infrared light traverse a translucent tissue bed (finger, earlobe); pulsatile changes are isolated and processed against a reference table to yield SpO<sub>2</sub>.

- Measures oxyHb, deoxyHb, metHb, and carboxyHb.
- SpO<sub>2</sub> 90% corresponds to PaO<sub>2</sub> ~60mmHg — the shoulder of the oxyHb dissociation curve; below this, small ↓ SpO<sub>2</sub> → large ↓ PaO<sub>2</sub>.

## Oxyhaemoglobin Dissociation Curve

### Right Shift (↓ Hb–O<sub>2</sub> affinity → ↑ O<sub>2</sub> delivery to tissues)

Causes:
- ↑ H<sup>+</sup> (acidosis)
- ↑ Temperature
- ↑ PaCO<sub>2</sub>
- ↑ 2,3-DPG

### 2,3-DPG

| ↑ 2,3-DPG | ↓ 2,3-DPG |
|---|---|
| Uraemia | Hypophosphataemia |
| Cirrhosis | Banked blood |
| Hypoxaemia / anaemia | Acidosis |

## ECG Monitoring

- Standard 5-lead configuration; monitor leads II and V5 concurrently.
- >85% of ischaemic events affecting the LV during surgery are detected by ST-segment analysis of leads II and V5.
- Inadequate anaesthetic depth suggested by: ↑ HR, ↑ BP, tearing, sweating.

## Arterial Line

- Provides beat-to-beat haemodynamic data.
- Arterial waveform morphology and pulse pressure variation assess intravascular volume status.

## Central Venous Pressure

- CVP catheter estimates right atrial and RV pressures.
- Reflects RV preload; less reliable as a surrogate for LV filling or fluid responsiveness in isolation.
## Pulmonary Artery Catheter (PAC)

- PAC passes through RA → RV → pulmonary artery branch.
- Balloon inflation occludes proximal pulmonary artery → measures distal (back) pressure.
- This pressure = pulmonary artery wedge pressure (PCWP) = pulmonary artery occlusion pressure (PAOP) = reflects **left atrial filling pressure**.


## Capnography (ETCO<sub>2</sub>)

- Normal ETCO<sub>2</sub>: 33–45mmHg.
- During GA: PaCO<sub>2</sub>–ETCO<sub>2</sub> gradient typically ~5mmHg.
	- In absence of significant V/Q mismatch or sampling error: ETCO<sub>2</sub> 35mmHg ≈ PaCO<sub>2</sub> 40mmHg.
- ETCO<sub>2</sub> is a function of: CO<sub>2</sub> production + alveolar ventilation + pulmonary circulation.

## Depth of Anaesthesia — Bispectral Index (BIS)

- BIS monitor analyses the EEG signal via a processed algorithm → single dimensionless number.
- Scale: 0 (isoelectric EEG) → 100 (fully awake).
- Target range for GA: **40–60**.

---

## Neuromuscular Monitoring

Three stimulation patterns:
1. **Single twitch** — single supramaximal stimulus.
2. **Train-of-four (TOF)** — 4 stimuli at 0.5sec intervals; TOF ratio = T4/T1 height.
3. **Tetanic stimulation** — continuous stimulus.

### Interpretation

- TOF ratio <0.7 → non-depolarising block present.
- TOF ratio ≥0.9 → adequate recovery from relaxant.
- Reversal: spontaneous recovery (time) or anticholinesterase (e.g. neostigmine) — (see [[Muscle Relaxants]]).

---

## Urine Output

- Aldosterone, ADH, renin–angiotensin II, and ANP all ↑ in response to surgical stress → salt and water retention.
- Urine output is negligible under GA without a fluid load; low UO alone is not diagnostic of hypovolaemia intraoperatively.

MAP = DBP + ⅓(SBP – DBP)
