## Mechanism of Action

1. Decreases the rate of disociation of [[01 - Anaesthetics/GABA]] from the [[GABA-A receptor].

![GABA](01%20-%20Anaesthetics/GABA.md)

2. Inhibits [[01 - Anaesthetics/NMDA Receptor]] ([[Glutamate]] receptor). This receptor is responsible for Calcium ion influx that initiates a cascade required for [[Neuroplasticity]] and [[Memory formation]].

3. [[Antiemetic]] effect via enhanced [[01 - Anaesthetics/GABA]] activity on [[5-HT3 receptor]]s in the [[Chemoreceptor trigger zone]].

## Effects

### [[Central nervous system]]

### [[Cardiovascular system]]

- Drop in [[Blood pressure]] due to [[Sympatholysis]]-mediated [[vasodilation]].
- Also [[venodilation]] leading to a drop in [[Preload]].

### Respiratory

- Dose dependent depression of ventilation
	- Typically apnoiec after induction dose
- Obtunds airway reflexes (good for [[supraglottic airway devices]])

## Practical Points

- Draw up aseptically to avoid bacteria contamination (as there is no preservative)
- Causes pain on infiltration, can mix with a few mls of lidocaine 1%
- Rate of induction greatly effects dose in the brain (see )
